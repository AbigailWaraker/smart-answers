Student finance calculator

This is an estimate of your student finance. You must apply to find out exactly what you’ll get.

Student finance

You could get per year:

  • £6,000 Tuition Fee Loan

  • £2,872 Maintenance Loan (for living costs)

  • £3,387 Maintenance Grant (for living costs)

Extra student funding

You could get:

  • up to £155.24 a week (1 child) or up to £266.15 a week (2 or more children) Childcare Grant
  • up to £1,573 per year Parents’ Learning Allowance

  • Child Tax Credit

  • up to £2,757 per year Adult Dependant’s Grant

  • Disabled Students’ Allowances

  • University and college hardship funds (extra help with costs while studying)

  • NHS Bursary (NHS funding towards your fees and living costs)

Your result is an estimate. Usually, you only get student finance if you’re doing your first higher education qualification - check if you qualify.

Extra help

You could get a bursary or scholarship from your university or college.

You might be able to get help with the costs of travel for study or work placements as part of your course.

Use the Family Action grant search to check whether you qualify for funding from a charitable trust.

Student loans have to be paid back - grants, bursaries and allowances don’t.

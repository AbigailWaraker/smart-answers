Your partner’s National Insurance record and your State Pension

You can’t increase your State Pension through your spouse or civil partner’s National Insurance contributions.

The new State Pension is based on your own National Insurance record.

If you’re widowed or you become widowed

You might be able to inherit a portion of your late spouse or civil partner’s Additional State Pension or Protected Payment if you become widowed and you were married or in a civil partnership before 6 April 2016.

What you might be able to inherit depends on when your spouse or civil partner reached or would have reached State Pension age.

If your partner reached State Pension age or died before 6 April 2016 you may be able to inherit a portion of their Additional State Pension.

If partner reached State Pension age or died under State Pension age on or after 6 April 2016 you may be able to inherit a portion of their Protected Payment.

You won’t be able to inherit either your late spouse or civil partner’s Additional State Pension or Protected Payment if you remarry before State Pension age.

You also might be eligible for Widowed Parent’s Allowance, a Bereavement payment or Bereavement Allowance if you haven’t yet reached State Pension age.

Increase your retirement income

You might be able to increase your State Pension amount if:

  • you’re eligible for National Insurance credits
  • you make voluntary National Insurance contributions
  • you choose to defer your State Pension

You can also contribute to workplace pension and personal or private pensions.

Contact the Pension Service for help and advice.
